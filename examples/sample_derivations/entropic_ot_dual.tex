\section*{Derivation: Entropic OT Dual}

Let $\mu, \nu$ be probability measures on a Polish space $\mathcal{X}$,
and let $c : \mathcal{X} \times \mathcal{X} \to \mathbb{R}$ be a cost function.
The entropic optimal transport problem is
\[
    \mathrm{OT}_\varepsilon(\mu, \nu)
    = \inf_{\pi \in \Pi(\mu,\nu)}
      \int c(x,y)\, d\pi(x,y)
      + \varepsilon\, \mathrm{KL}(\pi \,\|\, \mu \otimes \nu).
\]

Introducing Lagrange multipliers $f, g$ for the marginal constraints,
we can write
\[
    \mathrm{OT}_\varepsilon(\mu,\nu)
    = \sup_{f,g}\; \int f\, d\mu + \int g\, d\nu
      - \varepsilon \int \left( e^{(f(x)+g(y)-c(x,y))/\varepsilon} - 1 \right)
        d\mu(x)\, d\nu(y).
\]

Differentiating under the integral with respect to $f$ and setting the
derivative to zero, we get
\[
    \int e^{(f(x)+g(y)-c(x,y))/\varepsilon}\, d\nu(y) = 1
    \quad \text{for all } x,
\]
which gives the Sinkhorn fixed-point equation. Clearly, by symmetry the
same holds for $g$, so both potentials are determined up to a constant
and the iteration converges.

By Fubini, we can swap the integrals in the dual whenever needed,
and the change of variable $u = f/\varepsilon$ simplifies the
expression further. It follows that the optimal coupling has the form
$\pi^*(x,y) = e^{(f^*(x)+g^*(y)-c(x,y))/\varepsilon} d\mu(x)d\nu(y)$.
